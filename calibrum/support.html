Emergency

Calibrum is not a medical service. Stop using the app and seek emergency care if you experience any of these TRT red-flag symptoms:

  • Hematocrit greater than 55% on your most recent panel — risk of stroke / pulmonary embolism. Call your prescriber today.
  • Signs of deep-vein thrombosis or pulmonary embolism: sudden one-sided leg swelling, chest pain, shortness of breath, coughing blood — call 911 now.
  • Priapism (painful erection lasting more than 4 hours) — go to the emergency room immediately.
  • Severe estradiol crash with debilitating joint pain, dry skin, sudden complete loss of libido and erectile function — contact your prescriber within 24 hours.
  • Suicidal thoughts or self-harm: call 988 (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or your local crisis line. UK: Samaritans 116 123.

In any other US emergency call 911; outside the US use your local emergency number. Always call your prescriber for any concerning side effect.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Calibrum do?

Calibrum is an educational companion for adult men on a physician-supervised testosterone replacement therapy protocol. The app helps you log injections (testosterone cypionate / enanthate / propionate, anastrozole, hCG, ancillaries), rotate sites, photograph and decode your LabCorp / Quest lab PDFs, triage estradiol-related symptoms, calculate Free Testosterone with the Vermeulen formula, time your bloodwork around dose changes, and prepare a Doctor Visit Prep PDF for your urology or endocrinology appointment. The AI Coach ("Atlas") answers general questions about TRT 24/7 and cites the source for every claim.

Is Calibrum medical advice?

No. Calibrum is an educational companion — not a medical device. It does not prescribe medication, does not recommend dose changes, does not start or stop anastrozole, does not diagnose hypogonadism, polycythemia, prostate disease, or any other condition. Atlas explains what the Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guidelines, AUA TRT guidelines, AAFP TRT monograph, Bhasin 2018 meta-analysis, TRAVERSE trial, and FDA prescribing information say about TRT, and explicitly redirects any personalised question to your prescribing physician.

Who is Calibrum for?

  • Men diagnosed with low testosterone managing TRT under a licensed prescriber
  • Patients of telehealth TRT clinics (Hone Health, Hims, Maximus Tribe, Marek Health, TRT Nation, Defy Medical)
  • Men curious whether they may be a TRT candidate (the Free Testosterone Calculator is free, no subscription)
  • Men tired of being dismissed by their primary-care doctor and looking for the right questions to ask their endocrinologist or urologist

Calibrum is not for anabolic steroid cyclers, bodybuilders running off-label testosterone, transgender HRT patients (who are better served by trans-specific apps and clinics), or women on hormone replacement therapy. The app is calibrated for medical TRT only.

Which medications does Calibrum support?

  • Injectable testosterone: testosterone cypionate, testosterone enanthate, testosterone propionate, Aveed® (testosterone undecanoate)
  • Topical / oral testosterone: AndroGel®, Testim®, Fortesta®, Testopel® (pellets), Jatenzo®, Kyzatrex®
  • Aromatase inhibitors: anastrozole (generic), Arimidex®, Aromasin® (exemestane)
  • Adjuncts: hCG (Pregnyl®, Novarel®, generic), DHEA, vitamin D, zinc, ancillaries you and your prescriber are using

How does the AI Coach (Atlas) work?

Type a question in the Coach tab. Calibrum first runs a retrieval step on your device against a bundled corpus of medical literature: Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guidelines on testosterone therapy in adult men, AUA TRT guidelines (2024), AAFP TRT monograph, Bhasin et al. 2018 meta-analysis, TRAVERSE trial, FDA prescribing information for testosterone and anastrozole, and 30+ peer-reviewed PubMed studies covering anastrozole, hCG, hematocrit management, and SHBG dynamics. The retrieved passages are sent with your message to DeepSeek to compose Atlas's reply, with citations. Every reply ends with "Educational, not medical advice. Consult your prescribing physician."

Will the AI Coach change my dose?

No. Atlas is forbidden by its system prompt from recommending a specific dose, telling you when to escalate or de-escalate, suggesting starting or stopping anastrozole, or comparing your situation to "what other guys do on Reddit". Dose decisions belong to you and your prescriber. Atlas can explain what the Endocrine Society guideline says about typical titration, what side-effect patterns are commonly described in the literature, and what specific questions to bring to your next visit.

What is Lab Snap & Decode?

Photograph or import your LabCorp, Quest, hospital, or telehealth-clinic lab PDF. Calibrum sends the image to OpenAI Vision (GPT-4o) via our Cloudflare Worker proxy with store: false for one-shot extraction of biomarker values — Total T, Free T, SHBG, Estradiol (E2), Hematocrit (HCT), Hemoglobin, PSA, prolactin, and any other markers on your panel. Calibrum then displays each marker against the published reference range and against personal-optimal-range bands derived from Endocrine Society guidance. It does not tell you to change your dose.

What is E2 Symptom Triage?

The most underserved part of TRT. Log mood, libido, energy, joint pain, water retention, nipple sensitivity, dry skin, etc., and Calibrum pattern-matches them against three profiles based on your last lab and your last dose change:

  • 🟢 Likely common / settling — practical context from clinical guidelines, plus what to watch.
  • 🟡 Worth flagging at your next visit — suggested questions for your prescriber.
  • 🔴 Stop & call your prescriber / seek emergency care — red-flag list above (HCT > 55%, DVT/PE signs, priapism, severe E2 crash).

The triage is grounded in the Endocrine Society guideline, AUA TRT guideline, and FDA prescribing information for testosterone and anastrozole, and is intentionally conservative.

What is the Free Testosterone Calculator?

The Vermeulen formula is the gold-standard calculation for Free Testosterone and Bioavailable Testosterone from Total T, SHBG, and Albumin. The calculator is free, no subscription required — the app's upstream funnel for men investigating whether they may be hypogonadal before they pay for the rest. The formula and reference bands follow Endocrine Society guidance.

What is Smart Bloodwork Timing?

Changed your dose 6 weeks ago? Calibrum tells you exactly when E2 stabilizes (typically 4–6 weeks for cypionate / enanthate, 1–2 weeks for propionate or daily SubQ) and reminds you to retest at the half-life-correct trough. The math follows pharmacokinetic data in the FDA prescribing information for each ester. We surface direct order links to at-home lab providers — disclosed as affiliate links when applicable.

What does the Doctor Visit Prep PDF contain?

An auto-generated, single-page summary of your last 90 days: every dose with its date and site, lab trend (Total T, Free T, E2, HCT, Hemoglobin, PSA), symptom severity log, and 3–5 prescriber-friendly questions tailored to your trajectory ("Ask about HCT trend if it crosses 52%", "Discuss switching from once-weekly IM to twice-weekly SubQ if E2 swings > 30 pg/mL"). Tap "Share" to AirDrop or email it to your urology or endocrinology office.

How is my data stored?

Injection logs, lab values, lab PDF photos, symptom entries, calculator inputs, Doctor Visit PDFs, and all chat messages live on your device only, in iOS storage protected by your device passcode and Apple's Data Protection. They are never uploaded to our servers. The retrieval index used by Atlas runs on-device too. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Can I use Calibrum offline?

Logging injections, lab data, symptoms, the Free Testosterone Calculator, the Doctor Visit PDF, and Smart Bloodwork Timing reminders all work fully offline. The AI Coach chat and Lab Snap & Decode require an internet connection because they call our Cloudflare Worker proxy to reach DeepSeek and OpenAI Vision. The on-device retrieval step still runs without internet — Atlas simply waits for connectivity to compose the reply.
