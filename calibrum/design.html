2. Mode strategy — system-following, chat dark-first

JTBD opening times span 2 AM (#1, #5) to Saturday morning (#4) to Friday afternoon (#7). Chat surface defaults DARK — that's where 2am moments live. Lab Decode and PDF preview can be light-dominant (paper/document aesthetic). Onboarding + paywall + dashboard follow iOS system. Don't force a single mode app-wide.

3. Tone anchor — calm competent friend who has read every endocrinology guideline

Not a doctor (not prescribing). Not a coach (no "let's crush it"). Not a wellness app (no "honor your journey"). Anchor to MARK at 2 AM: a 47-year-old father, sleepless, holding his phone, scared he's crashed his E2. Lowercase chat bubbles. Empathy line first → fact → citation → action option. Borrow Maximus's pragmatic-direct register; add the warmth Hims has and Hone lacks.

4. Anti-patterns (the never-list)

  1. Never use bright red as a primary alert color. JTBDs #1, #6, #7 are anxiety-already-elevated states; red weaponizes anxiety and triggers the over-correction loop the VidaVital VOC described.
  2. Never use exclamation marks in AI responses. They escalate emotional state at the exact moment we need to lower it.
  3. Never use "ALPHA / CRUSHING IT / OPTIMIZE / STAY ANABOLIC" copy. This loses MARK to TRT Plus.
  4. Never use Hims-style quippy humor about low T. MARK at 2 AM is not laughing.
  5. Never default to "consult your doctor" as a complete answer. The user already knew that — it's why he's here at 2 AM. Always pair "discuss with doctor" with a substantive RAG-grounded answer.
  6. Never weaponize the paywall on the panic surfaces (#1, #6). Paywall on the lab decode (#2) and the doctor PDF (#3) — these are the satisfaction peaks.
  7. Never auto-alert HCT/E2 into a notification badge until clinically warranted (HCT >54%, E2 outside protocol range for 2+ labs). Notifications must earn the user's trust.
