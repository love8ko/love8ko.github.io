Emergency

JabWell is not a medical service. Frequently Asked Questions

What does JabWell do?

JabWell is an educational companion for adults using a GLP-1 medication prescribed by a licensed healthcare provider — Ozempic®, Wegovy®, Mounjaro®, Zepbound®, Rybelsus®, Saxenda®, Victoza®, Trulicity®, or compounded semaglutide / tirzepatide. The app helps you log injections, rotate sites, track weight and side effects, hit a daily protein target, monitor hair and skin changes, detect plateaus, and prepare a doctor-visit summary. The AI Nurse ("Nora") answers general questions about GLP-1 medication 24/7 and cites the source for every claim.

Is JabWell medical advice?

No. JabWell is an educational companion — not a medical device. It does not prescribe medication, does not recommend dose changes, does not diagnose pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, gastroparesis, hypoglycaemia, or any other condition. Nora explains what the FDA prescribing information, AAFP, Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, and peer-reviewed studies say about GLP-1 medication, and explicitly redirects any personalised question to your prescriber.

Which medications does JabWell support?

All major GLP-1 receptor agonists and dual GIP/GLP-1 agonists used in adults:

  • Ozempic®, Wegovy®, Rybelsus® (semaglutide — Novo Nordisk)
  • Mounjaro®, Zepbound® (tirzepatide — Eli Lilly)
  • Saxenda®, Victoza® (liraglutide — Novo Nordisk)
  • Trulicity® (dulaglutide — Eli Lilly)
  • Compounded semaglutide and compounded tirzepatide from authorised telehealth pharmacies (Hims, Ro, Mochi, Henry Meds, Eden, etc.)

How does the AI Nurse work?

Type or speak a question in the Coach tab. JabWell first runs a retrieval step on your device against a bundled corpus of medical literature (FDA prescribing information for each medication, AAFP guidelines, Mayo Clinic and Cleveland Clinic patient education, ~30–50 peer-reviewed PubMed studies). The retrieved passages are sent with your message to DeepSeek to compose Nora's reply, with citations. Every reply ends with "Educational, not medical advice. Consult your prescriber."

Will the AI Nurse change my dose?

No. Nora is forbidden from recommending a specific dose, telling you when to escalate or de-escalate, or comparing your situation to "what other people do". Dose decisions belong to you and your prescriber. Nora can explain what the FDA prescribing information says about typical titration, what side-effect patterns are commonly described, and what questions to ask your prescriber at your next visit.

What is Side Effect Triage?

When you log a side effect (nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, fatigue, headache, sulfur burps, etc.) JabWell shows a three-tier verdict:

  • 🟢 Common — usually settles with practical tips from clinical guidelines
  • 🟡 Worth flagging at your next visit with suggested questions
  • 🔴 Stop and call your prescriber / seek emergency care with the red-flag list above

The triage is based on FDA prescribing information and AAFP guidance, and is intentionally conservative.

What is the Plateau Detector?

If your weight has been flat for 4+ weeks despite consistent injections, JabWell summarises the trend and gives you specific questions to discuss with your prescriber about titration timing, plateau physiology, and protein/strength training adjustments. It does not tell you to change your dose.

What is the Protein Coach?

GLP-1s suppress appetite, which makes losing muscle a real risk. Protein Coach calculates a daily protein target around 1g per kilogram of body weight and helps you log meals against it with quick suggestions tuned for the GLP-1 appetite curve (small, dense, easy to keep down). The 1g/kg target follows AAFP and dietetics literature for adults on weight-loss medication.

What is the Hair / Skin Photo Journal?

Roughly 25–35% of GLP-1 users report hair shedding or skin laxity (sometimes called "Ozempic face"). JabWell lets you take a weekly photo and (V1.1+) run an opt-in AI analysis to flag changes early so you can act with a dermatologist or your prescriber before it accelerates. Photos are stored on your device only.

What does the Doctor Visit PDF contain?

An auto-generated, single-page summary of your last 12 weeks: every dose with its date and site, weight trend, side-effect log with severity, daily-feel slider, and 3–5 prescriber-friendly questions tailored to your trajectory. Tap "Share" to AirDrop or email it to your prescriber's office.

How is my data stored?

Injection logs, weight, side-effect entries, photos, custom symptom labels, and all chat messages live on your device only, in iOS storage protected by your device passcode and Apple's Data Protection. They are never uploaded to our servers. The retrieval index used by the AI Nurse runs on-device too. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Can I use JabWell offline?

Logging injections, weight, side effects, photos, and the protein coach all work fully offline. The AI Nurse chat and Voice Mode require an internet connection because they call our Cloudflare Worker proxy to reach DeepSeek and Whisper. The on-device retrieval step still runs without internet — Nora simply waits for connectivity to compose the reply.

How do I cancel my subscription?

On your iPhone, go to Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions → JabWell and tap Cancel Subscription. You can also reach this from inside JabWell via Settings → Manage Subscription. Cancellation must be at least 24 hours before the end of the current billing period to avoid the next charge.

How do I restore my subscription on a new device?

Open JabWell, go to Settings → Restore Purchases while signed in to the same Apple ID that made the original purchase. The subscription will be restored within a few seconds.
