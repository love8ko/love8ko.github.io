1.6 Anti-patterns · 14 don'ts

  • Cycle-pink / Flo-magenta in primary brand — instant «teen-period-tracker» bucket. Banned.
  • Real acne / hirsutism close-ups in onboarding — aversive + compliance dignity. Illustrations only until disclaimer-accept.
  • Red / yellow severity stoplight — clinical-cold, kills warmth. Use sage-warm → rose-gold → deep rose-gold instead.
  • Anti-Metformin / anti-BC rhetoric, even for Maya. Neutral framing only.
  • Diagnosis-language — never «you have PCOS». Always «your AMH 5.8 maps to PCOS-typical range per ESHRE 2023».
  • «Cure» / «heal» hormonal imbalance in copy. Always «manage / track / understand».
  • Definitive fertility predictions — TTC mode gives window, not certainty.
  • Pure white / NHS-blue — pearl warmth = wedge against Flo / Clue gaslighting.
  • Cute period illustrations (uterus-with-eyes, dancing ovaries) — credibility-killer for Anna / Tanya.
  • Email-required onboarding — privacy-first. Sign-in with Apple optional, not required.
  • Cloud-by-default photo sync — local storage; iCloud opt-in only.
  • Identifiable minor faces on marketing / share-cards.
  • Gamification (XP, levels, confetti, «PCOS warrior!»). Adherence streak is the sole exception.
  • AI Coach answers without RAG citation — every answer cites ACOG / ESHRE / AAP / Endocrine Society / PubMed. No-citation = refusal pivot.
